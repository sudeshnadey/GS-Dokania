Benefits of Tax Exemption

Registration of the company as an NGO under the Companies Act 2013 allows the company to enjoy several taxation benefits, e.g. Companies can save money by registering NGOs under the Companies Act, 2013

Right To Acquire Assets

Once your organisation has been officially registered, you can acquire land, own fixed assets, and acquire liability under the common seal. Unregistered organizations cannot buy, hold or sell land.

No Capital Requirement

To be able to operate independently, there is no minimum capital requirement. The charity can provide direct funding to NGOs. This allows NGOs to operate independently without a larger share of capital.

Protection from Personal Liabilit

You can purchase, acquire and register assets and stakes under the NGO's name. This is a well-organized way to protect yourself from untoward events, such as divorce, foreclosure, bankruptcy or judgement debt.

Corporate Entity

Your organization's relationships with the community and transactions will be improved as a corporate body. An NGO can sue to enforce its legal rights or be sued by its registered trustees.

Stamp Duty Exemption

Section 8 companies are exempted under the Income Tax Act from stamp duty. This allows for more tax-saving options for the company. An NGO can be registered in three ways:
Trust Registration

Trust, also known as Charitable trust, is one of the options for registering an NGO. The “trustor” (or “settlor”) creates a legal entity called trust. They transfer assets to the second party, or “trustee”, for the benefit of the “beneficiary”. Trust registration online are created to support and help the most vulnerable sections of society. Any group of people can register a trust. In India, there are no laws that govern public trusts. However, states like Tamil Nadu and Maharashtra have their own Public Trust Acts under which people can get trust registration online as well.

Societies Registration

A society can be defined as a group of people united by the common goal of promoting science, literature, and useful information. Societies also work to create military orphan funds and maintain public museums and libraries. Societies are subject to the Societies Registration Act, 1861. To be eligible for tax amnesty, they must be registered with their respective state Registrar of Societies. You can go for online society registration if you wish to go for ngo registration India.

A Section 8 company can be described as a trust or society. A Section 8 Company’s objectives are to promote science, commerce and sports, social welfare, religion, charity, and environmental protection. They are registered under the Companies Act, 2013, for charitable purposes. They are more trustworthy among donors, government agencies and other stakeholders. What documents are required for the registration of an NGO?

The following documents are required to register NGO online before a registered deed can be delivered to an NGO

Register Trust
  • Particulars of the trustee and settlor (Self-attested copy Id and address proof and information about occupation).
  • Trust Deed for Proper Stamp Value
  • Photographs of the settlor and trustee.
  • PAN Card of Trustee or Settlor
  • Documentation is required for the Trust Registered Address. For rented property, a NOC from the Landlord must be obtained.
Register Societies
  • Name of the society
  • Attestation of working space
  • All 7 members must provide proof of identity
  • 2 copies of the Memorandum of Association & By-laws of Society.
  • Documentation is required for the Trust Registered Address. For rented property, a NOC from the Landlord must be obtained.
Section 8 Companies Registration

  • DIR-2 (Consent of directors) and ID & Address proof

  • Address proof
  • Email address and contact number
  • No objection certificate is necessary if the registered office isn’t being rented.
